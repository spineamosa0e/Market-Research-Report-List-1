<p><p>The High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) power supply market is primarily divided into two types based on polarity: Positive Polarity and Negative Polarity. Positive Polarity HVDC systems are characterized by a positive voltage potential relative to the ground, making them suitable for applications with a high reliability demand. Conversely, Negative Polarity HVDC systems utilize a negative voltage potential, often preferred for long-distance transmission due to lower losses and improved system stability. Both types are essential for efficient and sustainable power distribution.</p></p>
