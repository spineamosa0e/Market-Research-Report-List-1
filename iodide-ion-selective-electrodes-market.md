<p><p>Iodide ion selective electrodes (ISEs) are specialized sensors used to measure iodide concentration in solutions. The market for ISEs is segmented into three main types: crystal membrane, liquid membrane, and PVC membrane. Crystal membrane electrodes offer high stability and sensitivity using crystalline materials. Liquid membrane electrodes employ organic solvents to selectively transport ions, while PVC membrane electrodes utilize polyvinyl chloride to form a membrane that selectively interacts with iodide ions. Each type serves specific applications in environmental, biomedical, and industrial fields.</p></p>
