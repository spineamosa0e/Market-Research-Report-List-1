<p><p>Bone densitometers are devices used to measure bone mineral density, crucial for diagnosing osteoporosis and assessing fracture risk. The market is divided into two main types: axial bone densitometers and peripheral bone densitometers. Axial bone densitometers, primarily dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DXA) machines, assess central skeletal sites like the lumbar spine and hip for high precision. Peripheral bone densitometers, which include smaller, portable devices, evaluate distal sites like the wrist and heel, offering convenient screening options in various settings.</p></p>
