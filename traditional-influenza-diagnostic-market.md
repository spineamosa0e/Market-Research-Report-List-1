<p><p>The traditional influenza diagnostic market includes several key types. Rapid Influenza Detection Tests provide quick results using nasal swabs, ideal for immediate diagnosis. Viral Culture involves growing the virus from a sample in a lab, though it’s time-consuming. Direct Fluorescent Antibody (DFA) Tests detect viral antigens in respiratory specimens using fluorescence microscopy for reliable results. Serological Assays measure antibodies in the blood, offering insights into past infections but typically require more time for results. Each method serves distinct clinical needs.</p></p>
